TH

Thai people have lived with PM 2.5 issue for more than 20 years.

We all dream of a day where we live without it.

Why can’t we still find a way to solve the issue?

During the last PM2.5 season, my eyes were so irritated that I had to go to the hospital. The doctor used a sharp-edged tool and got something out of my eyes. He showed me what he just got rid of but I couldn’t see because it was so small. The doctor said it was a particle matter.

Tharathorn Intaratep told us about his story during his break time. As a motorcycle taxi driver, he spends between 14-16 hours on the road every day transporting people and delivering foods via different mobile applications. He has been working as a driver for many years since it gives him the freedom he longs for.

After that day, I had to wear an eye patch and couldn’t work for a couple of days. I still had to see a doctor every once in a while. They suggested I wear a mask and a helmet with an eye shield. However, I couldn’t always wear that since it won’t let me see clearly at night.

PM2.5 refers to particles that are thinner than a human hair (2.5 micrometers), and therefore cannot be detected by our respiratory system. It can travel directly into capillary air bags in the lungs. Short-term health effects include coughing, shortness of breath, and skin irritation. The longer we breathe in the air that contains PM2.5 that exceeds the limit, the higher chance we would develop serious diseases as the bodies receive deadly substances through PM2.5 such as heavy metals, carcinogen, and allergens. This is similar to what smoking does.

The World Health Organization revealed that every year 7,000,000 people die from air pollution.

And what is our Thai government doing about this?

Prevention

includes providing a Clean Room, a public space with air purifiers free of charge, installing air purifiers on the streets, giving knowledges and updating the current situation about PM2.5 for citizens via the mobile application Air4Thai & AirBKK, and closing schools temporarily on days that PM2.5 exceeds the standard level.

Active Cutback

limiting hours for trucks entering the central area of the city, agricultural burn, and constructions, reducing the chance of wildfires by promoting the use of dry leaves as products, teaming up to stop wildfires by allowing local authorities to handle the situation by themselves.

even though the Cabinet of Thailand has leveraged this issue as National Agenda that must be solved immediately since 2018.

6 out of 10 citizens in Thailand think that PM2.5 is an urgent issue and are not happy with the way the government is handling it,

source: Suan Dusit Poll 2020

lack of holistic approaches

This year, PM2.5 Committee has scheduled a meeting with 54 government agencies.

lack of budget

State Audit Office of the Kingdom of Thailand discovered that there are no tracking records of the works of over 1,000 funded Northern Wildfires Networks whereas the local communities living close to the forest are raising money to buy equipment to stop the wildfires.

lack of knowledge

Mae Hong Son full of PM2.5! Only one district gets an air quality checking!

lack of consistancy

National Plan to solve PM2.5 issue of 2021 was proposed to the Cabinet of Thailand only 1 month away from the PM2.5 season!

Bunnaroth Buaklee, Chiang Mai Breath Council

“

"First of all, you have to be clear what problems you’re dealing with. If you take PM2.5 issue seriously as if it’s a catastrophe, then you’ll only care about it temporarily. In fact, this issue about PM2.5 is actually like a sleeping giant. It’s related directly with the infrastructure of manufacturing sector and social behaviors, which would not be developed sustainably. We need changes in the manufacturing sectors all year round. This the kind of problem you can’t just complete short-term solutions in a few months."

”

Or should we have something specifically to solve PM2.5 issue?

Thai people are working together to drive a Clean Air Act

กฎหมายนี้มุ่ง…

1.

Recognize the right to breathe clean air as an official right of Thai citizens

2.

Be used as a new working tool for the existing government agencies as well as the new ones that focus only on the air quality control

3.

Allow citizens to access information about the air quality and enhace thai air quality to meet the international standards

4.

Create a source to fund maintenance by driving motivation and penalty fees in order to encourage all sectors to contribute to better air quality

source: 1. Drafted Holistic Governance of Clean Air for Health Act by Thailand Clean Air Network (Unofficial Name) 2. Drafted Management for Clean Air Act by Thai Chamber of Commerce and Network (Unofficial Name)

Imagine the day that the law of clean air is officially present… what would happen?

The law will support in the existing environment and other related laws and fulfill the right to breathe clean air. Also, it will give citizens more channels to call for justice, allowing them to "get the right they deserve right away".

Tharathorn , a motorcycle taxi driver , becomes sick seemingly due to the air pollution
Tharathorn can request a support for his right to breathe clean air. (section 15)
Tharathorn can consult a private organization who works to help vulnerable subjects for to conduct a lawsuit and claim damages in the form of a compensation.
The organization can also be a representative for other motorcycle taxi drivers according to the class action regulation.

note: For cross-border pollution cases coming from other countries, it is not the plaintiff who has to prove the cause of his or her sickness, instead it is the defendant who would have to provide evidences to protect themselves.

What do Thai officers think about this law?

Supakit Boonsiri, Deputy Director General of Department of Industrial Works

“

“I believe that there’s good intention behind this law. Department of Industrial Works’ job is to monitor and track sources of pollution with the use of existing legalized tools. Industrial sectors are well aware of the issue and are working closely to solve the problem together."

”

“

"I completely agree with the concept. But we all believe that one law should cover many issues. We’re driving the Promotion and Conservation of National Environmental Quality Act in order to fix all pollution-related problems, such as contaminated water, garbage, and air pollution. We’re going to include the content of Clean Air Act in this because all pollution-related issues deserve to be treated the same way."

”

Athapol Charoenshunsa, Director General of Pollution Control Department

“

"Although today’s Thai laws still don’t officially recognize the right to breathe clean air, the right to live and possess a healthy life, which are parts of the right to breathe clean air, are already included in the international laws about human rights. They are also fundamental rights recognized by Constitution of Thailand for a long time. There’s no excuse for the government to ignore the issue of air pollution. Not only we hope for the success it, but also the ongoing process that would allow Thai people to understand and become aware of their own rights to breath clean air.” Thailand Clean Air Network

”
