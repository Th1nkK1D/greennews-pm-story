Knowing the problem: the apperance

During the last PM2.5 season, my eyes were so irritated that I had to go to the hospital. The doctor used a sharp-edged tool and got something out of my eyes. He showed me what he just got rid of but I couldnt see because it was so small. The doctor said it was a particle matter.

Tharathorn Intaratep told us about his story during his break time. As a motorcycle taxi driver, he spends between 14-16 hours on the road every day transporting people and delivering foods via different mobile applications. He has been working as a driver for many years since it gives him the freedom he longs for.

After that day, I had to wear an eye patch and couldnt work for a couple of days. I still had to see a doctor every once in a while. They suggested I wear a mask and a helmet with an eye shield. However, I couldnt always wear that since it wont let me see clearly at night.

PM2.5 refers to particles that are thinner than a human hair (2.5 micrometers), and therefore cannot be detected by our respiratory system. It can travel directly into capillary air bags in the lungs. Short-term health effects include coughing, shortness of breath, and skin irritation. The longer we breathe in the air that contains PM2.5 that exceeds the limit, the higher chance we would develop serious diseases as the bodies receive deadly substances through PM2.5 such as heavy metals, carcinogen, and allergens. This is similar to what smoking does.

During his time of sickness, the PM2.5 level around his neighborhood has exceeded the limit for as long as 34 days.

PM2.5 issue happens naturally, so that means we can't do anything about it?

PM2.5 issue is caused by both natural and human factors.

the source of PM2.5

the weather and the geography of the area

Central, November - May

Once the season of PM2.5 arrives, what is our Thai government going to do?

Prevention

  • Providing a Clean Room, a public space with air purifiers free of charge
  • Installing air purifiers on the streets
  • Giving knowledges and updating the current situation about PM2.5 for citizens
  • Closing schools temporarily on days that PM2.5 exceeds the standard level.

Active Cutback

  • Limiting hours for trucks entering the central area of the city, agricultural burn, and constructions.
  • Reducing the chance of wildfires by promoting the use of dry leaves as products
  • Teaming up to stop wildfires by allowing local authorities to handle the situation by themselves.

Knowing the problem: the core

Experts have agreed on one thing; the solutions still lack of 4 elements.

Holistic approaches

This year, PM2.5 Committee has scheduled a meeting with 54 government agencies.

Budget

State Audit Office of the Kingdom of Thailand discovered that there are no tracking records of the works of over 1,000 funded Northern Wildfires Networks whereas the local communities living close to the forest are raising money to buy equipment to stop the wildfires.

Knowledge

Mae Hong Son full of PM2.5! Only one district gets an air quality checking!

Consistancy

National Plan to solve PM2.5 issue of 2021 was proposed to the Cabinet of Thailand only 1 month away from the PM2.5 season!

These elements are missing, resulting in PM2.5 being handled like it is one of the temporary catastrophes

Bunnaroth Buaklee, Chiang Mai Breath Council

"First of all, you have to be clear what problems youre dealing with. If you take PM2.5 issue seriously as if its a catastrophe, then youll only care about it temporarily. In fact, this issue about PM2.5 is actually like a sleeping giant. Its related directly with the infrastructure of manufacturing sector and social behaviors, which would not be developed sustainably. We need changes in the manufacturing sectors all year round. This the kind of problem you cant just complete short-term solutions in a few months."

Or should we have something specifically to solve PM2.5 issue?

Finding the solutions

Thai people are working together to drive a Clean Air Act

1.

recognize the right to breathe clean air as an official right of Thai citizens

2.

be used as a new working tool for the existing government agencies as well as the new ones that focus only on the air quality control

3.

allow citizens to access information about the air quality and enhace thai air quality to meet the international standards

4.

create a source to fund maintenance by driving motivation and penalty fees in order to encourage all sectors to contribute to better air quality

Data Source:

  1. Drafted Holistic Governance of Clean Air for Health Act by Thailand Clean Air Network (Unofficial Name)
  2. Drafted Management for Clean Air Act by Thai Chamber of Commerce and Network (Unofficial Name)

The act is on its way to the House of Representatives of Thailand for consideration to become an offical act.

Imagine the day that the act of clean air is officially present what would happen?

The law will support in the existing environment and other related laws and fulfill the right to breathe clean air. Also, it will give citizens more channels to call for justice, allowing them to "get the right they deserve right away".

Tharathorn, a motorcycle taxi driver, becomes sick seemingly due to the air pollution.

Tharathorn can request a support for his right to breathe clean air. (section 15)

Tharathorn can consult a private organization who works to help vulnerable subjects for to conduct a lawsuit and claim damages in the form of a compensation.

Tharathorn can also be a representative for other motorcycle taxi drivers according to the class action regulation, allowing the others to receive the same compensation.

Note: For transboundary pollution cases, it is not the plaintiff who has to prove the cause of his or her sickness, instead it is the defendant who would have to provide evidences to protect themselves.

Citizens are driving this clean air act forward. Do Thai officers agree with this?

I believe that theres good intention behind this law.
Department of Industrial Works job is to monitor and track sources of pollution with the use of existing legalized tools. Industrial sectors are well aware of the issue and are working closely to solve the problem together."

Supakit Boonsiri, Deputy Director General of Department of Industrial Works

Athapol Charoenshunsa, Director General of Pollution Control Department

"I completely agree with the concept. But we all believe that one law should cover many issues. Were driving the Promotion and Conservation of National Environmental Quality Act in order to fix all pollution-related problems, such as contaminated water, garbage, and air pollution. Were going to include the content of Clean Air Act in this because all pollution-related issues deserve to be treated the same way."

This act is only one of many tools
but one thing that Thai people have always had is the right to breathe clean air which is the very fundamental right regarding the wellbeing that comes with everyone from the first day they were born.

"Although todays Thai laws still dont officially recognize the right to breathe clean air, this right is already included as one of the fundamental rights recognized by Constitution of Thailand and other international countries for a long time. Theres no excuse for the government to ignore the issue of PM2.5.

Nevertheless, what we want to see most is how Thai people come to understand and become aware of their own rights to breath clean air.
